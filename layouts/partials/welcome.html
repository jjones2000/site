I am a Theoretical Physics PhD student at the University of Birmingham researching statistical physics and phase transitions.

I am currently working on a new kind of statistical physics perturbation theory - analogous to the quantum mechanical version and born from a recently discovered expansion of the (in)famous Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff formula. This breaks down the walls of fortified problems from the literature, allowing the properties and phase transitions of classical spin models to be probed.

I have a passive interest in computing and finance, having completed projects pertaining to machine learning, investment strategies and trading.
